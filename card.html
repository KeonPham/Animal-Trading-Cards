Siberian Tiger

The Siberian tiger or Amur tiger is a population of the tiger subspecies Panthera tigris tigris native to the Russian Far East, Northeast China and possibly North Korea.

  • Scientific name: Panthera tigris altaica.
  • Size: without tail – 160-200 cm, length of tail about 100 cm. Weight of an adult animal can reach 300 kg.
  • Population: Only about 50 wild Siberian (Amur) tigers remain in Russia and China today.
  • Habitat: Mountain forests of eastern Russia.

In appearance, the Siberian tiger is distinguished from other tiger subspecies by its muted orange coat accented by black stripes, its thick fur, and its size; it is considered by many experts to be the world’s largest tiger. Fewer than 600 Siberian tigers remain in the wild, while several hundred more are kept in zoos and nature parks worldwide.
